About Me

Introduction

I am an aspiring full stack web and mobile app developer. I am a very detail-oriented person who loves to build and modify high-quality physical and digital things. Problem solving is really rewarding for me and I have the acumen for it. I am always interested in learning new things and therefore value and persue on-going education. I also enjoy bicycling, swimming, and running for general fitness and life balance and as a means of engaging with the beauty of the outdoors. I absolutely love music and how it can augment your mood or bring you to a new one. Oh, and by the way, I really enjoy trying all different types of foods.

I strive to always produce the best that I can with whatever I engage in and believe that if everyone adopted this attitude that the world would be a better and ever-improving place.

Where I'm From

I am originally from Stamford Connecticut and now reside part-time both in South Salem New York and Coventry Rhode Island. I would ultimately like to reside full-time in Rhode Island or some similar place with easy access to the ocean which is my most favorite and necessary place in the world to be near.

I hold a Bachelor of Science degree in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Connecticut.

My dream job is to develop desktop and mobile-friendly applications that would allow users to accomplish things that are socially or personally important to them, and in a way which can only be provided by the technology available through the use of technologies related to the internet including the use of GPS information.

Background

During my undergraduate program in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Connecticut, I developed an interest in computer programming from exposure to it as part of various class projects. After graduating from the University of Connecticut, I utilized my engineering skills and programming interest and abilities, to design and perform kinematic analyses of noncircular gears and noncircular gear-driven mechanisms using independently written BASIC software and AutoCAD. I wrote data acquisition, editing, utility, and data processing software modules using DOS and Visual Basic for a mountain bike race timing service I created and for which my domain goodtiming.com was acquired. I gained some experience with Visual FoxPro programming and SQL while employed at Furman Selz in New York City. I further entertained my programming interest by taking several college classes in the Computer Information Systems program at Roger Williams University in Bristol, Rhode Island where I had some exposure to Visual Basic, SQL, Java, and web development. Following that, during subsequent employment, I authored a web site to service a yearly craftsman show which further underscored my interest in and curiosity about web development. I then decided to take several online courses covering HTML, CSS, JavaScript, AJAX, and PHP which led to the development of the various class and personal projects indicated in this page: http://www.goodtiming.com/aboutme/. As a result of those classes and development projects, I have at least touched on many of the components of full stack development using PHP on the back end. I am currently enrolled in thinkful.com's Front End Web Development course to provide further structure to my knowledge and fill in any gaps that I have in my front end skills.
